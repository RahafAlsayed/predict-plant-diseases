Project Details

Overview of the graduation project, model development process, and how the classification system works.

Graduation Project â€“ University of Jeddah

This project was developed by Data Science students from the University of Jeddah, under the supervision of Dr. Heba Fasihuddin.

The system aims to support plant health monitoring by providing an AI tool that analyzes leaf images and predicts the presence of plant diseases with high accuracy.

Model Development & Selection

Several deep learning models were trained and tested, including GoogLeNet, YOLO models, and MobileNetV3.

After experimenting with multiple architectures, feature tuning, and performance comparisons, two models were selected: MobileNetV3-Large and YOLOv11.

These models achieved the highest scores across accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score, making them the most reliable choices for deployment.

How the Web System Works

The user uploads an image of a plant leaf through the website interface.

The image is processed and passed to both deployed models: MobileNetV3-Large and YOLOv11.

Each model predicts the disease type (or healthy condition) based on training data. The system then displays the final classification result to the user, showing the detected disease name and health status.
